Chocolate Fudge

Description

This is a super simple chocolate fudge recipe you can make on the stovetop with just a few ingredients. It’s smooth, rich, and perfect for gifting or snacking.

Beginners can follow along easily: measure, melt, stir, pour, and chill. That’s it!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups semi‑sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 (14 oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: chopped nuts or sprinkles

Steps

  1. Line an 8×8 inch pan with parchment paper so the fudge is easy to lift out.
  2. In a small pot on low heat, add chocolate chips, condensed milk, and butter.
  3. Stir gently until everything is melted and smooth (do not boil).
  4. Remove from heat. Stir in vanilla and a pinch of salt.
  5. Pour the mixture into the pan. Smooth the top. Add nuts or sprinkles if you like.
  6. Chill in the fridge for about 2 hours, then cut into squares and enjoy!
Beginner Tip: If the chocolate looks thick or grainy, the heat might be too high. Keep it low and keep stirring.
